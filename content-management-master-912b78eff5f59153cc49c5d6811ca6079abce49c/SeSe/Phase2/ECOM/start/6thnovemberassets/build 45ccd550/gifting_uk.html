Weber life hacks: Winterregeln!

  • Seasonal tips – Ready? Before you start

    Winter has its own barbecue rules. Here are some very useful set up tips to start your barbecue with the right foot..

    Choose the right spot.

    Move your barbecue close to the house, but avoid overhangs, porches and everything that could present a fire hazard.

    Dress to barbecue:

    wrap up warm, but avoid long scarves and dangling tassels. Choose a tight jacket and wear barbecue gloves rather than woollen mittens. We recommend the Weber Premium Gloves.

    Light the area.

    Night is falling rapidly in winter. Keep an outdoor lamp or torch within reach.

    Special tip:

    Use the iGrill thermometer Thermometer iGrill to monitor the temperature or your food via the app so you can spend more time with your friends and family in the warm.

  • Seasonal tips – Set… Winter has its own barbecue rules

    If you follow a few easy steps, your grilling over this festive season is guaranteed to be enjoyable and delicious. Take a look at these tips we selected for you.

    Check your ash catcher or grease management system and remove all water or ice from inside it.

    With the cold your BBQ will need more time to heat up and may require more gas or charcoal. Start earlier, check you have enough fuel, and keep spare canisters or briquettes on the side.

    Keep the lid down as much as possible to avoid the heat escaping. Only open it against the wind.

    Special tip:

    Mit dem Use the iGrill thermometer to monitor the temperature or your food via the app so you can spend more time with your friends and family in the warm.

  • Seasonal tips – Go! Give it the final touch

    When barbecuing, every steps of the process is important for a delightful experience. Over the winter, this is especially true. Here are some seasonal tips to finish up your barbecue like a pro.

    Cooking time will be slightly longer as well. If the temperature is below 0°C, calculate 20% more cooking time.

    Side dishes, sauce and even oil don’t like the cold. Keep them inside or only bring them out at the very last moment.

    Keep dishes warm.
